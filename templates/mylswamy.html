Current Position: Director, National Institute of Aerospace Technology (NIAT)

Dr. Mylswamy Annadurai is an eminent Indian scientist who has played a pivotal role in shaping India's space exploration programs. As the Program Director for both Chandrayaan-1 (India's first lunar mission) and the Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan), he demonstrated exceptional leadership and technical expertise. His visionary contributions have not only advanced ISRO's capabilities but have also earned India international acclaim in space technology. Dr. Annadurai's career exemplifies how dedication and innovation can propel a nation to new heights in scientific achievement.

Key Achievements

  • Spearheaded Chandrayaan-1 (2008) as Project Director, marking India's successful entry into lunar exploration
  • Led the Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan, 2013) as Program Director, achieving India's first interplanetary mission
  • Served as Director of ISRO Satellite Centre (ISAC) from 2015 to 2018, overseeing numerous satellite projects
  • Honored with the Padma Shri award in 2016, India's fourth-highest civilian award
  • Recognized globally as the "Moon Man of India" for his contributions to lunar exploration

PSG College of Technology Legacy
